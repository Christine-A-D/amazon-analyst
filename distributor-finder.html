Overview
The problem it solves

"I’ve tried to open an account with about 100 distributors and in 95% of cases I was refused. And those from whom I was able to open a wholesale account, they do not have profitable products."

"But as I looked through their price list, their prices were to high when I compared to what it is selling on amazon."

Such statement can be found on Amazon discussions and reddit. One of the critical issues in Google searches is that it won't give you all the websites you are looking for due to its nature. Moreover, there are websites that only appear when you search for their company name, which is crucial since you only have keywords such as "B2B", "Wholesalers", and "Distributors" together with a location. Hence, such keywords limit what Google can provide. There are lead generating tools, however these will cost you another penny which would add to your costs and will not guarantee results due to the vagueness of their keywords.

How it works
Workflow
01
Finding reliable and good suppliers is the most challenging part
Price offers and invoice-related problems are one of Amazon’s key challenges. Plenty of wholesalers exist but few are legitimate with low price offers.
02
Use of two API to automate the ful pipeline
I generated code which uses Google Maps API to pull distributors or wholesalers based on keywords and location, which then passes the results through the Oxylabs API to interpret brands they offer from each distributor’s website.
03
Lessen hours in researching
The tool has the capability to scrape more than 1,000 distributors depending on the keyword and location.
04
Finding legitimate wholesalers with less time and effort
Such workflow saves time and effort. From more than 1,000 wholesalers/distributors scraped to less than a hundred when it passes through interpretation with relevant keywords: brands and categories. Hence large-scale research/scraping is one of the strategies it should undergo. Moreover, such techniques will not only scrape large-scale data but also save time relative to manual sourcing.
05
Technical and sourcing knowledge combined
This workflow shows the efficiency of automation and Amazon B2B distribution skills combined.
