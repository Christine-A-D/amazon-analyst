Overview
The problem it solves

Finding profitable brands, not just any brand, is one of the key factors in achieving high ROI.

Moreover, such calculations (e.g. actual weight and volumetric weight) are essential and should not be overlooked, as these vary and directly impact the final landed cost, if miscalculated or neglected, then it can result in a loss of profit.

How it works
Step-by-step process
01
Supplier cost input
The unit price from the supplier is entered in euros alongside the total units available. The spreadsheet calculates the total supplier cost automatically as the starting point for all analysis.
02
Shipping cost calculation
Shipping cost per unit by air or sea freight is factored in, giving a buy cost and landed cost in both euros and USD, accounting for currency conversion at current rates.
03
Tariff & landed cost
Import tariffs are applied on top of the landed cost to produce the true cost per unit excluding and including tariff which is a critical figure for cross-border sourcing decisions.
04
FBA fulfillment & referral fees
Amazon's FBA fulfillment fee and referral fee (15%) are calculated per unit based on the product's selling price and category, reflecting the true cost of selling on Amazon.
05
Profit per unit & ROI
The final output is a clear profit per unit and ROI percentage — calculated automatically from the selling price minus all costs. Results update in real time as any input changes.
